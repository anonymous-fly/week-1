\(\TeX\) macros for convenient shorthand

Another thing that’s super useful about \(\TeX\) are the macros.

Definition

Macro (Noun)

A macro is a convenient shorthand for a more complex piece of code.

Let’s say I plan to use this equation again and again \[ y = \sum_{i=1}^n {mx + \alpha} \]

I can just create a macro as follows:

Go into math mode and use \newcommand

\[ \newcommand{\mymacro}{y = \sum_{i=1}^n {mx + \alpha}} \]

Now I can do this:

\(\mymacro\)

and this:

\[\mymacro\] \[\color{blue}\mymacro\] \[\color{orange}\mymacro\]
